Siquijor, Philippines

Siquijor is a small island province in the Philippines, known for its beautiful beaches, waterfalls, and a bit of mystique! Here's the brief information of the province:

  • Location: Central Visayas region, south of Cebu.
  • Size: One of the smallest provinces in the Philippines.
  • Nicknames: "Isla de Fuego" (Island of Fire) due to the fireflies, and known for mystic traditions.
  • Capital: Siquijor
  • Things to do:
    • Relax on white-sand beaches (Salagdoong, Paliton)
    • Swim in turquoise waterfalls (Cambugahay)
    • Explore caves (Cantabon)
    • Visit the ancient Balete Tree
    • Snorkel or dive in coral reefs
  • How to get there: Ferries from Dumaguete City are the main access point.

Enjoy, Explore and Experience Siquijor.

Siquijor, known as the "Island of Fire," is a hidden gem in the Central Visayas region of the Philippines. Famous for its white-sand beaches, turquoise waters, and mystical folklore, the island offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Visitors can explore enchanting waterfalls, centuries-old churches, and mysterious caves while enjoying a laid-back island atmosphere. Siquijor is also renowned for its traditional healing practices, where local shamans use ancient rituals and herbal medicine. Top attractions include Cambugahay Falls, Salagdoong Beach, the Old Enchanted Balete Tree, Lazi Church and Convent, Cantabon Cave, and Mount Bandilaan Nature Park. Whether you seek adventure, relaxation, or a touch of the mystical, Siquijor promises an unforgettable experience.
