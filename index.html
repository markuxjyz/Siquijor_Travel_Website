Step into a world of wonder where nature’s beauty meets age-old mysticism. Siquijor, the Island of Fire, beckons you with its cascading waterfalls, pristine beaches, and legendary charm. Things to Do in Siquijor

Siquijor offers a mix of mystical experiences, from visiting faith healers and exploring old churches to enjoying pristine beaches, diving spots, and cascading waterfalls.

Experience Traditional Healing Rituals

Discover Siquijor’s mystical side by visiting local healers who use age-old techniques, medicinal herbs, and spiritual rituals to provide wellness and balance.

Try Exciting Water Activities

Beyond snorkeling, explore the island’s vibrant marine life through scuba diving, paddleboarding, and kayaking in its crystal-clear waters.

Indulge in Local Delicacies

Savor Siquijor’s culinary delights such as fresh seafood, traditional lechon, and the famous torta (a sweet and dense local pastry).

Chase Stunning Sunsets

Head to Paliton Beach or the shores of San Juan and watch as the sun paints the sky with breathtaking hues of orange, pink, and purple.

Enjoy, Explore and Experience Siquijor.

Siquijor, known as the "Island of Fire," is a hidden gem in the Central Visayas region of the Philippines. Famous for its white-sand beaches, turquoise waters, and mystical folklore, the island offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Visitors can explore enchanting waterfalls, centuries-old churches, and mysterious caves while enjoying a laid-back island atmosphere. Siquijor is also renowned for its traditional healing practices, where local shamans use ancient rituals and herbal medicine. Top attractions include Cambugahay Falls, Salagdoong Beach, the Old Enchanted Balete Tree, Lazi Church and Convent, Cantabon Cave, and Mount Bandilaan Nature Park. Whether you seek adventure, relaxation, or a touch of the mystical, Siquijor promises an unforgettable experience.
